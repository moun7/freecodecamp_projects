Michael Faraday

The father of electromagnetism

 

Michael Faraday was a british scientist who rose to prominace during the 19th century. He is most famous for his contributions to electrochemistry, specifically Faraday's constant and Faraday's law of induction. Some of his other notable contributions were the discovery of benzene, synthesis of certain organochlorides, development of an early form of oxidation numbers, and the invention of the bunsen burner.

Faraday's Law of Induction

Specifically, Faraday's law states:

The emf induced in a circuit is proportional to the time rate of change of the magnetic flux linking that circuit.

Or in other words, electricity can be induced in a circuit by changing the surrounding magnetic environment. This has had tremendous consequences on the scientific world and society as a whole, specifically with regards to the generation of electricity and electromagnets, as changing the magnetic environment is as simple as moving or rotating the magnet!
